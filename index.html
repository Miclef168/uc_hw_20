Case Study Overview

Time Span: 3 Weeks

Overview: Design a mobile application to meet a growing pain point of users with a focus on usability and heuristics.

Problem Description: More pet owners are looking to provide safe and nutritious food for their pets. However, information on the internet is unreliable at best. gourPet is a mobile application that provides articles, products, and recepies that can be tailored for specific pet diets. All provided information is verified by veterinarians for credibility.

My Role: User Research and Analysis, UX Writing, Wireframes, Prototyping and User Testing

Collaborators: Kennedy Brophy, Nikki Hanacek

Research and Analysis

Methodology: Five user interviews were conducted over two days, and an open survey was sent out by social media for one week.

Significant Findings:

  • 58.2% of users interviewed and surveyed have conducted additional research beyond veterinarian reccommendations for information on pet diets.
  • 54.5% of users interviewed and surveyed consider information found internet untrustworthy.

User Quote 1

“So I found myself doing a lot of research on this and finding a lot of frankly, just crap information, a lot of noise, very little signal, a lot of marketing that was kind of fake news, a lot of marketing that didn't have any scientific backing to the claims of what you should feed your pets.”

User Quote 2

"We were like really low on food and then we were trying to refill my vet was like, oh yeah, we don't carry it anymore. And the manufacturer was like thinking about not making it anymore. So we had to have the vet help us find like the alternative brand. But it was really stressful because like, we're rationing food."

Ideation

Affinity Diagram Results: Users pain points were primarily focused around cost, accuracy of information, and availability of product.

Feature Prioritization: Based on the findings, the decisions was made to focus on access to product information, the ability to customize information based on pet type, and alternative diet options such as homemade recipes. Users did not express enough intrest in research articles to make the feature a prioroty, but one worth including in the wireframed design.

Wireframine

Sketches: We initially started with several simple sketches meant to give us a framework. Early on, we decided to go with a bottom navbar in order to provide a simple interface where users could use one finger to interact with the application. It was important for us to make this as easy to use as possible, which led to significant chances to the design of the app as we iterated on our designs.

From Concept to Prototype

The User Profile

The user flow for our profile set up needed to allow the user to customize pet profiles as well, in order to take advantage of the custom searches for dietary information that would be provided. It was decided to have an onboarding process not only for the application, but for creating a pet profile as well. We wanted to be able to allow the user to feel as though the app was doing everything it could to make infromation for their pet as transparent and detailed as possible.

From Concept to Prototype

Information Searching

We initally split up the search options for recipes, articles, and products, wanting to allow the use to know they were searching for the right information. Users would also be able to favorite and save recipes, articles, and products in order to be able to find them easier. We had chosen a recipe book icon for the recipes, but it was later removed after user testing.

Iterating the Navigation

Aftr the initial round of user testing, we quickly discovered we were increasing the cognitive load of the users by separating the search functions. Users indicated they had no idea what the recipe book was used for. It was a great lesson in pattern recognition and subtractive thinking. We combined the search functions into the magnifying glass icon and created a nav that would allow the user to switch between search modes. This also had the benefit of simplifying the nave bar.

Additional changes were made, but this was by far the most significant.

Reflections and Next Steps

After this project, the idea of subtractive thing was made clear, and my team and I realize the age old saying "less is more". While the project was only three weeks, it was a valuable experience in avoiding assumptions, or at least always testing assumptions with research. As far as next steps, my team decided to practice and experiment with branding and style. It was not apart of the challenge, but a basic color palette was created and applied to take some of the lo-fi wireframes to mid-fi.
